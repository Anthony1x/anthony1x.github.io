Why I choose not to have children, and why I think it's always better to adopt

I'll admit, my previous ramble on this topic sucked. It was written when I was really angry about something I honestly can't even remember anymore and all it was was several paragraphs of me blaming people that chose to bear children for all that is bad in the world. I apologize for the incoherence and I owe it to myself to make myself more clear, because I still stand behind it.

--------------------------------------------------------


"If we shouldn't breed dogs while there are dogs to adopt, why should we breed humans while there are humans to adopt?"

(Source)

This quote has been occupying my mind for a while now and it perfectly encompasses my thoughts on the topic of natalism, so I wanted to start my ramble with it.

Let's start from the beginning. I identify myself as an antinatalist. Fundamentally, that means that I follow the philosophical viewpoint that assigns negative value to birth. I believe it to be better not to exist at all. If I could choose right now, I'd rather never've been born at all.

Why is that?

Now, let me begin with something reasonable. For a loving parent, I'd think it's perfectly normal for them to want to give their child the best life it could possibly have. They don't want it to experience trauma. They don't want it to be bullied at school. They don't want it to be kidnapped or worse. They will do everything in their might to make sure this does not happen, whatever may be the case.

Unfortunately, we do not live in an environment where such awful occurrences do not happen. You could be a perfect parent to your child and it will, without doubt, experience injustice. It will be hurt. It will be treated unjust at some point in their life. Those are things that every human being is guaranteed to experience.

And that's just the best-case scenario. Bad people exist. Your child could also be a victim of bullying. Your child could be the victim of murder, or sexual harassment, or even things not influenced by humans, like illnesses. If you're optimistic, you might think "Oh, that's not gonna happen". And you might be right. But what if you aren't? What if your child is born blind? What if your child gets abducted, gets harmed, and will be traumatized for the rest of its life? That'd be fucking awful. Of course it would be. Not a single, good-willed person would want their child to go through that. Yet the chances of exactly that happening are not nil.

You might still make the best out of the situation, but that child you have will still have to go through it, having a harder time than everyone else.

This is what I mean when I say you're "gambling" with your child's life. Because your child did not choose to be here. Your child is the product of a chemical process in your brain, wanting you to reproduce. But now it's here, and it can not choose to stop existing. It will have to live with the pain.

I don't see the point in that.

Of course I am not looking at the bright sight of things. Of course good things happen in life. Your child could, through all hardships, die a peaceful death with its spouse and two healthy children by its side, not regretting a single thing, not wanting to die yet.

Of course your child could make the world a better place. It could cure diseases. It could get world-famous. It could turn out to be the highest-regarded person in history, having taken all the right paths, leading to it being remembered as the best thing to happen to human civilization 'till the end of time. Though it's reasonable for you to expect this will never happen, all things considered. I'm just looking at the other extreme of the spectrum here.


...OR your child could turn out to be one of the "bad people" I've talked about earlier. Your child could turn out to be a sociopath. Your child could turn out to be a mass-murderer. Your child could turn out to be a rapist. Your child could turn out to be the next Adolf Hitler.


Again, I'm just looking at extremes here. What I'm saying is that there is always a chance. A chance I do not want to take. I am not ready to birth a child into this place when I know I cannot protect it from the evil of earth, or protect it from turning to the evil of earth, even if I try my best to make sure this does absolutely not happen. It can happen and it has happened more than often enough.

And I'm not even considering the environment right now. I'm assuming there's going to be absolutely nothing wrong with you, the parent, or your family. Not every child is fortunate enough to grow up with two loving parents. Lots of children have to grow up without a father figure. Lots of children have to grow up, knowing their parents don't love each other no more, always hearing them fight. You may not want your child to know about it, but, well, it's kind of inevitable, considering it will live with you for 18 years at the low end.

You could be the reason your child grows up to be corrupt. One of the "bad people". And it will also give birth to children, making it experience the same things it had to experience as a child, and the cycle will continue.

Why risk that?




...I think you've got the point. But oh, it doesn't end here. Now let's consider how it impacts you, the parent.

Wanting your child to grow up as perfectly as it can means you giving up your own life. Always taking care of the damn kid means you not being able to do what you want to do. Obviously, I don't have children, so I wouldn't know, but every parent can tell you that having a child is like having another full-time job, except you don't get paid, YOU're the one that pays. And it's not just money, it's also your valuable time, and your valuable nerves. This is more up to you. Personally, I don't want a child to hinder my path to self-realization. I want to get as high up there as possible, and spending this many resources on a child WILL hinder your personal growth.

Oh and don't even start if you think you need a child in order for you to grow. That's just straight-up selfish. Don't have children for selfish reasons. Remember that it didn't choose to exist, you took that choice away from it. You're placing another existence on earth so that you can grow; just... don't. Please.


Now let's get into the most personal part of this ramble.

I am, just like all of y'all reading this, just a human. Good day. I'm 18 years old, and I still have my entire life ahead of me. And it's a damn understatement for me to say that I'm fucking tired of living. I honestly cannot wait for my life to end. I do NOT want to spend the next 50~ years of my life working, being another cog in this late-stage capitalistic society. I've not even started and I'm already tired of existence.

But please don't misunderstand. I'm not suicidal. And, though I still have to figure out what it means to have depression, I'm pretty sure I don't have depression. I also see it as natural, not wanting to be "just another cog". Making a child means making yet another cog. If my child thinks like me, it will also not want that. It would also rather not exist. Obviously I don't know if that will be the case, I can only speak from personal experience here. So I will not risk my child not having wanted to be born.

"The idea of having to 'earn a living' implies that, by default, you don't actually deserve to be alive."
(Source/)

This finally brings me to my last point. Let's say you agree with my points. You don't want your child to go through all that, but you'd still like to have children. This is where adoption comes in.

If your child is related to you by blood or not, it don't fuckin matter if you ask me. Your blood does not make you special, you're just one of billions of people.


When you decide to have a child, it does not get the choice to say "no". With orphans, well, they already kinda exist. They exist, not getting to live a normal life like all the other children. And it's not even their fault. Their parents could've been assholes, their parents could've died in a car accident, the reason does not matter, what matters is that there is a child wanting to live a normal life (or maybe not, but that's not the point) and it can't. So, instead of taking care of your biological child, take care of this orphan, that did not get to live normally. You don't burden another life with hardships, you just take one that already exists and make it... well, experience the hardships. One child less that has to suffer and one child more that gets to experience the love and affection of a parent. Everybody wins. Even if your biological child experiences a perfect life, that orphan you didn't adopt still has to suffer.


Once again, you wanting to reproduce is just chemicals in your brain, trying not to make your species go extinct. Fortunately for us humans, we are the only species that is self-aware of that. And as such, you can fight that instinct. So do. Say "no!" to the horny chemicals. You have been gifted a mind capable of making that choice, so do the world a favor and do that. It's for the best.

...oh, and if you're legally not allowed to adopt... maybe that means you also shouldn't have children at all, depending on the situation. I honestly made no research into the process of adopting, I've been told it's kinda hard, but it's more than worth it if you ask me.


I would love to hear your opinions on this, so if you have any thoughts on the matter, please do let me know. I spent quite the time writing this to make sure my points are clear, so I would love to know what you think.
